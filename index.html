The question gap

The commodity is the answer.
The scarce good is the quality of the question.

01

Answers got free

Frontier models can reason through almost anything you put to them. The supply of competent answers is effectively infinite — and effectively worthless.

02

Questions didn't keep up

The gap between what AI can reason through and what we actually ask it to is widening. Most of us prompt for a verdict, take it, and move on.

03

The muscle atrophies

Every time you outsource the thinking instead of the typing, the part of you that does the thinking gets a little weaker. Centaur is the friction that keeps it strong.

The signal

Two ways to use a mind like this.

Centaur reads every turn of the conversation and places it on one axis. Not to grade you — to make a usually-invisible habit visible while you can still change it.

Enhancing

You're doing the thinking. The AI is sharpening it.

  • + You bring a real tension, not a vague topic
  • + You explain your reasoning — the because, not just the what
  • + You engage the pushback instead of conceding it
  • + You name your own assumptions out loud
  • + You leave with a harder question than you came with

Offloading

The AI is doing the thinking. You're collecting the output.

  • – You ask for a yes/no verdict on a decision
  • – You want the answer written, not the problem understood
  • – You ask "what's the best approach" before defining the problem
  • – You're seeking validation, not examination
  • – "Just tell me what to do."
The behavior-change mechanic

It coaches the question, not just the answer.

When your prompt is shallow, Centaur doesn't quietly work around it. It names what's missing, offers a sharper version, and tells you why — so eventually you ask that question before you ever open the app.

You
"I'm thinking of launching a new product line. Is it a good idea?"
Centaur
That question is looking for a verdict — and I don't think a verdict is what helps you here. A more productive one: What's the scenario where this fails, and why haven't you ruled it out yet? That's where your actual thinking needs to happen.

Guided modes

Proven reasoning frameworks, run as a dialogue.

Start open-ended, or launch a structured session built around a way of thinking that's worth borrowing.

MODE 01

First Principles

Break the problem down to its irreducible truths, then reason back up from bedrock.

MODE 02

Pre-Mortem

It's a year from now and the plan failed completely. What happened — and why didn't you see it?

MODE 03

Devil's Advocate

Centaur argues the strongest case against you, seriously, until your position has earned its keep.

MODE 04

Inversion

Work backward from the outcome you most want to avoid to find what to stop doing now.

MODE 05

Five Whys

Iterative root-cause analysis. Each "why" peels back one more layer of the real explanation.

Why "Centaur"

In chess, a centaur is a human and an engine playing as one — and for years that pairing beat both the best humans and the best machines alone.

The lesson wasn't that the machine was smart. It was that the human stayed in the loop — directing, questioning, deciding.

The fastest way to lose that advantage is to stop thinking and start collecting answers. Centaur is built to keep you in the loop — to make the offloading visible the moment it starts.
