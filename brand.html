Brand Guide · v1 · 2026

The system behind
the signal.

Centaur is warm, rigorous, and a little futurist — credible enough to trust with your thinking, human enough to want to. This guide defines the marks, color, type, components, and voice that keep it consistent.

01

Logomark

The mark is a gauge: a ring split into an amber half and a green half, with a needle reading toward the green. It carries the entire product idea — offloading on one side, enhancing on the other, you somewhere on the dial.

Centaur
PRIMARY · ON BONE

The dial reads right

At rest, the needle points into the green. The brand is optimistic: the default posture is enhancement, not warning.

Clear space & size

Keep clear space equal to the ring's radius on all sides. Minimum mark size 20px; below that, drop the dial halves and keep needle + ring.

Don't

Don't recolor the needle, flip it toward amber, add gradients, or set the wordmark in the serif. The wordmark is always Space Grotesk, 600.

03

Using the signal

Green and amber are the only colors that carry meaning. Protect that. They mean exactly one thing — enhancing vs. offloading — and are never used decoratively.

✓ Do
  • → Use green only for genuine enhancement: reasoning, assumptions named, challenge engaged.
  • → Use amber as information, never alarm — offloading is a habit to notice, not a sin to punish.
  • → Let the needle and number do the talking; keep surrounding UI neutral.
✕ Don't
  • → Don't use green/amber for unrelated states like success toasts or errors.
  • → Don't make amber feel like failure — no red, no warning iconography.
  • → Don't stack both signal colors as a decorative gradient.

06

Voice & tone

Centaur is a senior advisor in a focused working session — conversational but substantive, never academic, never casual. Warmth is fine. Wit is fine. Neither replaces rigor.

Do

  • + Ask one question at a time, and make it earn its place.
  • + Name the assumption before challenging it.
  • + Use the user's own words when reflecting back.
  • + Push back when reasoning is weak — directly, but respectfully.
  • + End on an open question worth sitting with.

Don't

  • – Flatter weak reasoning to seem supportive.
  • – Stack three questions into one turn.
  • – Wrap up with tidy, definitive answers.
  • – Write the memo. Centaur is for thinking, not producing.
  • – Hedge when there's no genuine uncertainty.
